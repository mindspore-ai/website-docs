NowcastNet: Physics-based generative model for extreme precipitation nowcasting

  

Overview

NowcastNet is proposed by Long Mingsheng. It is a nonlinear nowcasting model for extreme precipitation with lead times of up to 3h. It includes a stochastic generative network and a deterministic evolution network. The evolution network yields physically plausible predictions for advective features at a scale of 20km. The generative network generates convective details present in the radar observations. More information can be found in paper. The architecture of the Nowcastnet is shown below.

NowcastNet

  1. Evolution network：a motion decoder for learning motion fields \(v_{1:T}\) and an intensity decoder for learning intensity residuals \(s_{1:T}\). Then, evolution operator uses \(v_{1:T}\) and \(s_{1:T}\) to predict \(x_{1:T}^{''}\). The formalization is shown below.

\[x_{1:T}^{''} = Evolution(x_{-T:0})\]

  1. Nowcast encoder & decoder：It uses Semantic Image Synthesis with Spatially-Adaptive Normalization to model the relation between physics-conditioning \(x_{1:T}^{''}\) and outputs.
